A journey, not a classroom.

Every young person moves through four stages with us. Each one is hands-on, supported, and built around real curiosity.

01

Discover

We start with curiosity, exploring industries, talents, and interests through interactive sessions that open doors students never knew existed.

See it in action →
02

Learn

Hands-on workshops and classes in real, in-demand skills like coding, design, communication, and finance, taught by mentors who do the work.

See it in action →
03

Build

Students apply what they learn to real projects and portfolios, building confidence and proof that future employers and schools look for.

See it in action →
04

Launch

With mentorship, résumés, and interview practice, every young person leaves ready for their next step, knowing they are not doing it alone.

Backed by educators

Built with the people who
know students best.

Elementary school counselors

Career discovery should begin with curiosity.

Lead counselors LaTesha Newell and Nikka Aghazadeh emphasized that upper-elementary students are developing their interests and sense of self. Early career exploration builds awareness, confidence, goal-setting, and stronger connections between school and real-world opportunities.

High school teachers

Students need career readiness before graduation.

Heritage High School educators highlighted CTC’s versatility, the importance of career-readiness skills before postsecondary education, and the need to reduce the stress students experience when making career choices.
